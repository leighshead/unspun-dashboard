How It Works: End-to-End System
1
Article Ingestion
News articles are automatically collected from trusted sources via News API and manually curated
‚è± 15-20 min daily
  • API Connection: NewsAPI with 70,000+ sources globally, updated hourly
  • Manual Filter Setup (Phase 0-1): Admin selects keywords, sources, domains before articles are pulled
  • Topic Filters: immigration, economy, climate, healthcare, tech policy, social issues, election coverage
  • Source Diversity: Left-leaning (NYT, WaPo, Vox), Right-leaning (Fox News, WSJ, National Review), Neutral (AP, Reuters, BBC)
  • Quality Checks: Must be recent (24-48 hrs), substantial length (500+ words), verified publisher
  • Data Storage: Raw HTML + cleaned text stored in PostgreSQL with metadata (source, author, publish date, URL)
  • Curator Role: Human reviews API results daily, selects 3-5 articles with high bias potential for annotation
  • Example Filters: "immigration AND border" from conservative sources, "climate change AND crisis" from progressive sources
2
Sentence Parsing & Preparation
Articles are broken down into individual sentences and loaded into the annotation tool
‚è± < 1 second automated
  • NLP Parsing: Custom sentence tokenizer splits article text while preserving quotes and complex punctuation
  • Unique IDs: Each sentence receives a UUID for tracking through the annotation pipeline
  • Context Preservation: Stores paragraph position, surrounding sentences, article metadata
  • Annotation Tool Loading: Parsed sentences appear in custom web interface with clean, readable formatting
  • Batch Processing: Can parse 10+ articles simultaneously in background queue
  • Quality Filters: Removes ads, bylines, photo captions, social media embeds‚Äîonly analyzes core editorial content
  • Output Format: JSON structure with sentence text, position, metadata ready for human or AI annotation
3
Bias Analysis & Annotation
Human annotators identify biased language patterns using a sentence-level taxonomy
‚è± 30-45 min per article (Phase 0-1) | < 1 min with AI (Phase 2+)
  • 6 Bias Types Taxonomy:
  •   1. Loaded Language: Emotionally charged words that reveal perspective (e.g., "slammed," "crisis," "hero")
  •   2. Source Imbalance: Only quoting sources from one political side
  •   3. Speculation as Fact: Presenting opinions or predictions as established truth
  •   4. Omission: Missing context or counterarguments that would change the narrative
  •   5. Framing: How the story is angled to emphasize certain aspects over others
  •   6. Clickbait Headlines: Sensationalized titles that misrepresent article content
  • Phase 0-1 Workflow: Human annotators read each sentence, click to tag bias type, highlight specific trigger words, add explanation note
  • Parallel Annotation: Two independent annotators review same article. Inter-annotator agreement must hit 90%+ before publishing
  • Disagreement Resolution: Third annotator reviews conflicts. If still uncertain, article is discarded
  • Phase 2 AI Workflow: Claude API processes article, suggests bias tags with confidence scores. Human reviews/corrects AI output in 5-10 min
  • Quality Control: All annotations tracked, weekly calibration meetings, annotation guidelines constantly refined
4
Card Generation & Content Creation
Transform annotations into visual bias report cards optimized for social media distribution
‚è± 15-20 min with AI assistance
  • AI-Powered Draft: Claude API receives annotation JSON, generates punchy headline + 2-sentence summary + key bias explanation
  • Copy Variants: Creates platform-specific versions (Twitter: 280 char, Instagram: visual-first caption, LinkedIn: professional tone)
  • Design Template Application: Automated insertion into pre-built Canva/Figma templates with brand colors, fonts, layout
  • Visual Elements: Bias type badges, severity indicators, source logos, sentence highlights overlaid on card
  • Legal Compliance Check: AI paraphrases article content to avoid copyright issues. Never quotes more than 2 consecutive sentences.
  • Human Review Loop: Content lead approves headline tone, verifies accuracy, adjusts copy for clarity (10-15 min)
  • Transparency Viewer Link: Each card includes QR code or short link to microsite with full sentence-by-sentence breakdown
  • A/B Test Variants: Generate 2-3 headline options, track engagement to optimize future cards
5
Publishing & Distribution
Content is distributed across social platforms with transparent links to sources and methodology
‚è± 5 min (Phase 0-1) | Automated (Phase 2+)
  • Multi-Platform Publishing: Simultaneous posts to Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn, Threads using Buffer or native APIs
  • Transparency Viewer: Interactive microsite where users click sentences to see bias tags, explanations, and methodology notes
  • Three-Layer Model: Social card (quick visual) ‚Üí Viewer (detailed breakdown) ‚Üí Original article (full context)
  • Engagement Tracking: Monitor comments, shares, saves across platforms. Flag constructive criticism for methodology refinement.
  • Chrome Extension Integration (Phase 1): Published cards automatically appear in extension's "Recent Analysis" feed
  • Substack Cross-Posting: Weekly roundup of top bias cards with deeper methodology explanations
  • Analytics Dashboard: Real-time tracking of reach, engagement rate, click-through to transparency viewer, subscriber growth
  • Feedback Loop: User corrections or disputes feed back into annotation quality review process

Three-Layer Transparency Model

Layer 1: Bias Cards (Social Media) ‚Äî Quick visual summaries showing what bias was detected and why. Paraphrased content, legally compliant.

Layer 2: Transparency Viewer (Microsite) ‚Äî Interactive page with color-coded sentence highlights, tooltips explaining each bias type, and detailed methodology notes.

Layer 3: Original Source ‚Äî Direct link to the original article so readers can verify coverage themselves and see full context.

Phase 1: Human-First Annotation (Months 1-3)

Strategic Objectives

Primary: Build a high-quality training dataset of 500+ annotated articles while establishing annotation excellence. Two independent annotators review each article to ensure consistency and catch edge cases. Target: 90%+ inter-annotator agreement before transitioning to AI-first approach.

Secondary: Launch Chrome extension (Months 2-3) to enable in-context bias highlighting directly on news websites. This eliminates the "link in bio" problem and provides seamless educational experience for users.

Manual Article Selection Process (Phase 0-1)
Why Manual Selection Matters
In Phase 0-1, we manually curate articles to ensure high-quality training data. NewsAPI provides access to 70,000+ sources, but not all articles are good candidates for bias analysis. Manual selection lets us focus on articles with clear educational value, diverse viewpoints, and important topics.

Monday Morning: Article Curation Workflow

1
Configure NewsAPI Filters
Set search parameters to surface relevant articles
‚è± 10-15 min
  • Topics: Choose 2-3 focus topics for the week (immigration, climate policy, healthcare reform, tech regulation)
  • Keywords: Specific phrases like "border security", "climate bill", "Medicare expansion"
  • Sources: Mix of left/right/center outlets (NYT, Fox News, WSJ, Reuters, AP)
  • Date Range: Last 24-48 hours for timely coverage
  • Language: English only for Phase 0-1
2
Review Feed Results
Scan 20-30 articles for bias potential
‚è± 20-30 min
  • Read headlines and opening paragraphs
  • Look for: loaded language, framing differences, source imbalances
  • Prioritize articles on same event from different outlets (great for comparison)
  • Flag articles with clear educational value
  • Note: articles that are purely factual (sports scores, weather) are NOT good candidates
3
Select Final Candidates
Choose 5-7 articles for annotation
‚è± 10 min
  • Diversity: Mix of left/right/center sources
  • Topics: 2-3 different topics (don't focus only on one issue)
  • Bias Types: Variety of bias patterns (loaded language, framing, source imbalance)
  • Length: 500-1500 words (short enough to annotate in 30-45 min)
  • Quality: Well-written articles from reputable outlets
4
Prioritize for Annotation
Rank top 3-5 for immediate annotation
‚è± 5 min
  • Which articles have clearest bias signals?
  • Which would make best educational content for social?
  • Which topics are most timely/relevant this week?
  • Save ranked list for Tuesday-Wednesday annotation
